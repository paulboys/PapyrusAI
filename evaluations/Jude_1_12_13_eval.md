## Model: qwen2.5

*Response time: 2713.8s*

## Translation

**12** These are reefs in your love, feasting fearlessly and shepherding themselves. Like waterless clouds driven by winds, like autumnal unfruitful trees uprooted twice.
**13** Like wild waves of the sea foaming up their own shame, wandering stars kept in darkness for an age.

## Notes

- **Verse 12:**
  - "Reefs" (σπιλάδες) is used metaphorically to describe false teachers who are like dangerous obstacles.
  - "Feasting fearlessly" (συνευωχούμενοι ἀφόβως) suggests that these individuals act with boldness and arrogance in their teachings.
  - "Shepherding themselves" (ἑαυτοὺς ποιμαίνοντες) indicates that they are not guided by the Holy Spirit but are self-serving.

- **Verse 13:**
  - "Wild waves of the sea foaming up their own shame" is a vivid image to describe the destructive nature and the consequences of these false teachers.
  - "Wandering stars" (ἀστέρες πλανῆται) refers to astral bodies that move unpredictably, symbolizing the erratic and deceptive behavior of false prophets.

## Model: llama3.2

*Response time: 933.5s*

## Translation

**12** These are the unrepentant ones who indulge in love feasts with you in vain, who shepherd themselves instead of others and have no concern for the holy Spirit. They are clouds without water, carrying only wind; trees that are twice dead and uprooted.

**13** They are waves of the sea by nature, foaming up to destroy souls, stars that wander for all eternity in darkness, kept captive by the chains of darkness.

## Notes

- In verse 12, "σπιλάδες" (reef) is more likely to be interpreted as a metaphor for hypocrisy or false piety, rather than an actual physical reef.
- The phrase "ἐγείρουν τὰς ἑαυτῶν αἰσχύνας" (they bring up their own shame) in verse 13 may suggest that these individuals are responsible for the spiritual condition of others.

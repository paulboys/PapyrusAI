# John 3:16

## Greek Text

## Translation

**16** Thus God loved the world in such a way that he gave his only son, so that everyone who believes in him may not perish but have everlasting life.

## Notes

- **"Οὕτως γὰρ ἠγάπησεν ὁ Θεὸς τὸν κόσμον,"** translates to "Thus God loved the world." The use of "thus" (οὕτως) emphasizes a specific manner or way in which God's action was executed.
- **"ὥστε τὸν Υἱὸν τὸν μονογενῆ ἔδωκεν,"** means "in such a way that he gave his only son." The aorist tense (ἔδωκεν) conveys a completed action, and the phrase "his only son" is rendered as "his unique son" to maintain natural English phrasing.
- **"πᾶς ὁ πιστεύων εἰς αὐτὸν,"** translates to "everyone who believes in him." The article before πιστεύων ("the one who believes") is omitted as it is generic and implied by the context.
- **"μὴ ἀπόληται"** means "may not perish," reflecting a negative subjunctive mood (V-2AMS-3S). The translation uses "perish" to convey the sense of ultimate destruction or death, which aligns with common English usage in this context.
- **"ἀλλ᾽ ἔχῃ ζωὴν αἰώνιον,"** translates to "but have everlasting life." The contrast is highlighted by "but," indicating a positive outcome despite the negative possibility of perishing. The term "everlasting" (αἰώνιος) emphasizes the eternal nature of this life.

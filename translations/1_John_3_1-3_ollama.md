# 1 John 3:1-3

## Greek Text

**1** Ἴδετε ποταπὴν ἀγάπην δέδωκεν ἡμῖν ὁ Πατὴρ, ἵνα τέκνα Θεοῦ κληθῶμεν, καὶ ἐσμέν. διὰ τοῦτο ὁ κόσμος οὐ γινώσκει ἡμᾶς, ὅτι οὐκ ἔγνω αὐτόν.
**2** Ἀγαπητοί νῦν τέκνα Θεοῦ ἐσμεν, καὶ οὔπω ἐφανερώθη τί ἐσόμεθα. οἴδαμεν ὅτι ἐὰν φανερωθῇ, ὅμοιοι αὐτῷ ἐσόμεθα, ὅτι ὀψόμεθα αὐτὸν, καθώς ἐστιν.
**3** καὶ πᾶς ὁ ἔχων τὴν ἐλπίδα ταύτην ἐπ᾽ αὐτῷ ἁγνίζει ἑαυτὸν, καθὼς ἐκεῖνος ἁγνός ἐστιν.¶

## Translation

**1** Behold what love the Father has given us, that we should be called children of God; and we are such. For this reason, the world does not know us because it did not know him.
**2** Beloved ones, now we are God's children, but what we will be has not yet been revealed. We know that when he is manifested, we will be like him, for we will see him just as he is.
**3** And everyone who has this hope in him purifies himself, even as he is pure.

## Notes

- **1:1 "Behold" (ἰδετέ)**: This word serves to draw the reader's attention and can be rendered as "behold" or "look at." Here, it emphasizes the importance of what follows.
- **2:3 "Beloved ones" (ἀγαπητοί)**: The term is a vocative form meaning "beloved," which indicates a personal address. It's best to keep this in the translation for the emotional impact.
- **2:4 "Children of God" (τέκνα Θεοῦ)**: This is a direct reference to our status as God's children, emphasizing our divine origin and relationship with Him.
- **3:1 "What love" (ποταπὴν ἀγάπην)**: The word ποταπή can mean "what kind of," suggesting the nature or quality of the love given. A literal rendering is appropriate here to maintain the emphasis on the quality.
- **3:1 "For this reason" (διὰ τοῦτο)**: This phrase introduces a causal link, explaining why the world does not know us.
